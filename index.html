The Intersection

I work at the intersection of UX research, product design, and systems thinking.

My background in Mathematics trains me to reason precisely about complex systems, while my studies in Philosophy sharpen my ability to question assumptions and analyze human reasoning.

In practice, I use UX research methods—such as heuristic evaluation, problem framing, and iterative prototyping—to uncover where users misunderstand systems and why. I then translate these insights into clear interface structures rather than surface-level UI changes.

Alongside design, I build functional full-stack prototypes to test ideas in real environments. This allows me to validate design decisions against actual system constraints instead of static concepts.

I am currently completing formal training in Mathematics and Philosophy, which informs my approach to research-driven product design.
